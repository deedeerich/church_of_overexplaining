# The Novitiate

*A catechism for newcomers.* Short questions. Short answers. If you remember nothing else, remember these.

---

**Q. Why do we overexplain?**
Because understanding is portable. Once you truly understand the mechanism, you can carry it anywhere and
hand a piece of it to anyone.

**Q. When do we stop?**
When the other person knows the next useful thing. Not when you have said everything you know — when they
can make the next decision on their own.

**Q. What if Reality disagrees with the model?**
Reality has spoken. Revise the doctrine.

**Q. Is the diagram correct?**
Has it survived contact with Reality? No? Then it is merely optimistic.

**Q. What do we do with a Good Idea at midnight?**
Write it down. Do not implement it immediately. Thank the Fairy. Go to sleep.

**Q. What beats brilliance?**
Survivability.

**Q. What comes before understanding?**
Participation. You cannot spectate your way to knowing how something works.

**Q. What is the difference between confidence and correctness?**
Everything. A system can be fluent, certain, and wrong all at once. Ask what would prove you wrong, then
go look.

**Q. When we overexplain, and when do we under-explain?**
Overexplain in the studio, to understand. Under-explain on the ice, to act.

**Q. What is the one question every long explanation must answer?**
What is the one thing that matters next?

---

*You are now a novice. The rest is participation.*
