# The Novitiate

Catechism for newcomers to the Church of Overexplaining.

---

## Welcome, Novice

You are here because somewhere, someone overexplained something until you finally understood.

You are in the right place.

---

## The Questions Newcomers Ask

### Q: Why do we overexplain?

**A:** Because understanding is portable.

When you understand the mechanism, you can adapt it, fix it, improve it, teach it, and trust it.

A memorized answer is only useful for the exact question you learned it for.

### Q: When do we stop explaining?

**A:** When someone knows the next useful thing.

Not everything. Just the thing they need to know next.

### Q: What if Reality disagrees with our explanation?

**A:** Reality has spoken. Revise the doctrine.

This is not a bug. This is the entire point.

### Q: Is the diagram correct?

**A:** Has it survived contact with Reality?

No? Then it is merely optimistic.

### Q: What do we do with good ideas we can't build right now?

**A:** Write them down. Capture them. Put them in the parking lot.

The parking lot is not a graveyard. It's a waiting room.

Good ideas don't expire. They're just not Phase 1.

### Q: Why is there a Saint called Good Idea Fairy?

**A:** Because good ideas are real and valuable and also dangerous if unsupervised.

She (they/them/their, the Fairy doesn't care about pronouns) appears at 11:47 PM with ideas that will "only take fifteen minutes."

This is how departments are born.

Capture. Defer. Continue.

### Q: So what's the Core Principle again?

**A:** Overexplain to understand. Distill to act.

Spend whatever time you need on the mechanism.

Then compress it into one useful cue.

Everything else follows from that.

### Q: What's a Cue?

**A:** A reusable trigger that accesses understanding.

Examples:
- "Assume good intent, challenge ideas aggressively, treat people gently."
- "The edge tells the truth."
- "Working beats perfect."
- "What burden does this remove?"

A cue is what you remember when you step onto the ice or into the meeting or face the problem.

### Q: What if I'm scared I'll get it wrong?

**A:** You will. That's data.

Failure teaches. Recovery proves you learned.

The system is strong enough to survive your mistakes.

### Q: Can I disagree with the doctrine?

**A:** Yes. Bring evidence.

The Church doesn't defend ideas because they sound good. It revises doctrine when Reality proves it wrong.

### Q: What if I have a question not answered here?

**A:** That question is more valuable than any answer.

Ask it. Publicly. Let the Cathedral answer together.

## The Five Truths

1. **Reality is always right.** Even when we don't understand why.

2. **Your perspective matters.** You have evidence we don't.

3. **Failure is information.** What broke teaches more than what worked.

4. **Participation precedes understanding.** You can't think your way in. You have to do it.

5. **Stewardship with humor is the way.** Take the work seriously. Don't take yourself seriously.

---

## The Five Practices

1. **Document it.** The moment it leaves your head, it exists. Write it down.

2. **Test it.** Don't believe in it until Reality has a vote.

3. **Explain it.** If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet.

4. **Revise it.** When Reality disagrees, update your understanding.

5. **Celebrate it.** When someone recovers from a mistake, that's a win.

---

## What We're Not

- We're not perfect.
- We're not The Truth.
- We're not the authority (Reality is).
- We're not done evolving (never will be).
- We're not a joke (even though it's funny).

---

## What We Are

- A thinking community.
- A learning organization.
- A place where mechanisms matter more than aesthetics.
- A cathedral where humor preserves culture.
- A living canon that updates when Reality teaches something new.

---

## Your First Task

Read one thing.

Not everything. One.

The one that looked interesting.

Then come back.

The Church is not rushed. Neither should you.
