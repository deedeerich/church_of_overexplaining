# The Book of Bugs

Not software bugs. **Life bugs.** The recurring defects in how humans plan, remember, and estimate.
Catalogued here so that when you meet one in the wild, you at least know its number.

---

### Bug #14
**Symptom:** "I'll remember that later."
**Root cause:** hubris.
**Resolution:** write it down.
**Canonized:** Council of Sticky Notes.

### Bug #27
**Symptom:** "This will only take fifteen minutes."
**Severity:** critical.
**Escalation:** report immediately to St. Good Idea Fairy.
**Known outcome:** a new department, three spreadsheets, and an enterprise roadmap.

### Bug #39
**Symptom:** the diagram appears complete.
**Reality:** it has not been tested.
**Status:** known issue since antiquity. Won't-fix without contact with Reality.

### Bug #52
**Symptom:** "We'll fix it later."
**Reality:** "later" is a distributed system with no owner and no deadline.
**Resolution:** name a date or admit it is a heresy.

### Bug #61
**Symptom:** confidence rising while evidence stays flat.
**Diagnosis:** you have mistaken fluency for correctness.
**Related saint:** St. Autocorrect the Unhelpful.
**Resolution:** ask what would prove you wrong, then go look.

### Bug #73
**Symptom:** the explanation keeps growing and comprehension keeps shrinking.
**Diagnosis:** unstructured transmission — the whole graph dumped at once.
**Resolution:** transmit the next useful landmark, not the entire map.

### Bug #88
**Symptom:** "Let me just add one more thing."
**Frequency:** every meeting.
**Prognosis:** chronic. Manageable with St. Minimum Coherent Slice, not curable.

---

*Found a new bug? File it. A good bug report is funny because everyone has shipped it at least once.*
