# Parables

The saints are the *what*. The parables are the *why it stuck*. Short stories that carry the
philosophy forward long after you have forgotten which commandment it was.

## The Parable of the Sidewalk Cracks

A person set out to walk a long way and vowed never to step on a single crack. They watched their
feet the whole journey and arrived exhausted, having seen nothing, and stepped on a crack anyway on
the last block.

The next person set out and simply kept walking. They stepped on some cracks. They also saw the
whole street.

*Moral:* continuity matters more than isolated perfection. A system that keeps going and occasionally
stumbles beats a system that optimizes one thing and sees nothing.

## The Good Idea Fairy and the Forty-Seven Tabs

At 11:47 PM the Good Idea Fairy whispered, *"What if it also did this?"* And it was good. So a tab was
opened. And the Fairy whispered again, and again, and by morning there were forty-seven tabs, three
half-built prototypes, and the original task untouched.

*Moral:* capture the inspiration without letting it derail today's work. The Fairy is real and often
right. She simply does not own the roadmap.

## The Architect Who Drew Too Many Boxes

An architect drew a diagram so complete that every possible case had a box, and every box had a line
to every other box. It was beautiful. It was also unmaintainable, and no one — including the architect,
six months later — could say what it meant.

*Moral:* every abstraction has a maintenance cost. Every diagram can be improved by one fewer box.

## The Two Coaches

One coach taught only fundamentals: edges, balance, repetition. The other taught only artistry:
expression, story, feeling. Each thought the other was wrong. The student who learned from both became
better than either could have made alone.

*Moral:* rigor and artistry are not opposing forces. Mastery is the marriage of the two.

## The Fall and the Recovery

A performer fell. The fall was recorded as data — a thing that happened, without explanation. Then they
got up, adjusted, and continued. *That* was recorded as data worth celebrating.

*Moral:* we do not invent explanations for the fall. We celebrate observable resilience. The recovery is
the part worth studying.

## The Diagram That Looked Better Than Reality

There was a diagram so elegant that the team preferred it to the actual system. When reality and the
diagram disagreed, they trusted the diagram. Reality, being authoritative, eventually collected the debt.

*Moral:* do not mistake a coherent diagram for reality. An untested diagram is merely optimistic.
