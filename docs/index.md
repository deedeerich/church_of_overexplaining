## The Four Pillars

### Reality
The only infallible authority. Everything else is commentary.

### Participation
Understanding comes through doing. Get on the ice. Get in the arena. Build the thing.

### Adaptive Coherence
The mechanism by which systems remain themselves while changing.

### Survivability
Long-term viability beats short-term brilliance.

## Core Doctrine at a Glance

**The Twelve Commandments** (start with these):
1. Reality outranks opinion.
2. Models serve Reality, never replace it.
3. Curiosity precedes certainty.
4. Build the smallest coherent thing first.
5. Expand only after coherence.
6. Participation is mandatory for understanding.
7. Failure is data.
8. Every system owns its consequences.
9. Preserve optionality.
10. Explain enough, but not more than the listener can absorb.
11. Every diagram must earn its existence.
12. Never trust St. Good Idea Fairy after 9 PM.

## Why We Overexplain

Overexplaining is not the disease — **unstructured transmission** is. Hold the whole graph
internally; transmit the next useful landmark. The goal of an explanation is not to prove how much
you know, but to transfer just enough of the right mental model that someone else can make the next
good decision on their own. [Read the full doctrine →](doctrine.md)
