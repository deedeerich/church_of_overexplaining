# The Book of Gratitude

Not jokes. Not doctrine. Just thanks.

Because after all the saints and heresies and feast days, the Church is not actually about
overexplaining. The overexplaining is the liturgy. The **religion is stewardship with a sense of
humor** — taking care of ideas, systems, communities, and people well enough that they survive long
enough for someone else to build on them.

So this page is for the people who quietly make systems work, and are almost never thanked for it.

---

Thank you to the **coaches** — who say the one-sentence cue that took a decade of understanding to earn.

Thank you to the **maintainers** — who keep the thing running long after it stopped being interesting.

Thank you to the **parents and volunteers** — who drive, who show up early, who hold the whole thing
together without a title.

Thank you to the **people who write the documentation** — the real kind, the kind a future stranger
will actually use.

Thank you to the **people who test the backups** — who find out whether recovery works *before* it has
to.

Thank you to the **people who sharpen the skates, reset the room, and fix the small broken thing** —
the invisible labor that everything visible depends on.

Thank you to the **person who asked the naive question** — and made the whole room understand it better.

Thank you to the **one who said "I don't know"** out loud, and made it safe for everyone else to.
