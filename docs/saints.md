# Saints and Archangels

The canonized principles of the Church. Saints are patterns that kept showing up until we had to
give them names. Archangels are the enduring forces behind them.

## The Archangels

The forces that do not change.

- **Archangel Curiosity** — the First Explorer. Carries a lantern into unknown caves. Asks the first question.
- **Archangel Stewardship** — guardian of what has been built; protector of future possibility.
- **Archangel Participation** — patron of builders, learners, and anyone in the arena. *"Get on the ice."*
- **Archangel Wonder** — patron of the reason any of this was worth understanding in the first place.
- **Archangel Recovery** — patron of getting back up; the fall is data, the recovery is worth celebrating.
- **Archangel Continuity** — patron of the thread that survives across breaks, machines, and versions.
- **Archangel Play** — patron of the joke that makes the true thing memorable.

## The Canonized Saints

### St. Good Idea Fairy 😈✨
**Patron of:** "What if…", scope expansion, accidental projects, brilliant ideas at 11:47 PM.
**Prayer:** *"This should only take five minutes."*
**Feast Day:** the day someone says *"This should only take fifteen minutes."*
**Observed outcome:** a new department, three spreadsheets, and an enterprise roadmap.
**Doctrine:** cannot be trusted unsupervised. Write the idea down. Do not implement it immediately.

### St. Kintsugi 🏺✨
**Patron of:** recovery, iteration, learning from mistakes, and not throwing away useful things because
they are imperfect. The crack, filled with gold, is part of the object now.

### St. Adaptive Coherence 🍄🌲
**Patron of:** making the pieces fit, reorganizing after reality changes, and keeping participation alive
through chaos.

### St. Controlled Chaos ⛸️⚙️
**Patron of:** complex architecture, family projects, and somehow making it work.
**Motto:** *"We meant to do that."*

### St. Minimum Coherent Slice ✂️
**Patron of:** shipping, architecture reviews, and the prevention of slide-deck crimes.
**Prayer:** *"Show me the smallest useful version."*

### St. Reality Check 🪞
**Patron of:** testing assumptions, killing beautiful bad ideas, and humility.
**Feast Day:** immediately after production deployment.

### St. Loop Viability 🔁
**Patron of:** recovery velocity, incident response, and getting back up after a fall.
**Motto:** *"Continue."*

### St. Progressive Disclosure 📚
**Patron of:** not overwhelming executives, not overwhelming engineers, not overwhelming anyone.
**Miracle:** explained a distributed system in three slides.

### St. Survivability ⚙️
**Patron of:** enduring architectures, operational excellence, and things that keep working.
**Doctrine:** survivability beats brilliance.

### St. Tangent of Context 🧭
**Patron of:** the belief that no answer has ever suffered from *slightly* more background.
(Held in careful tension with St. Minimum Coherent Slice. They are not enemies. They are a marriage.)

### St. Footnote the Thorough 📎
**Patron of:** the rabbit hole that was, in fact, worth exploring.

### St. "One More Thing" 🎁
**Patron of:** *"One last observation…"* — followed by another three pages.

### St. Autocorrect the Unhelpful 📱
*(Canonization in progress.)* **Patron of:** confidence without correctness. Depicted holding a phone that
has just replaced "goofy" with "goody" immediately before a serious discussion. The reminder that a system
can be fluent, certain, and wrong all at once.

## The Canonization Process

A pattern is canonized as a Saint when **all four** are true:

1. It appears independently in **three separate conversations** (not one clever moment).
2. It **survives contact with Reality** — tested against a real problem, not just asserted.
3. It is **still useful six months later**.
4. It makes everyone laugh because it is **painfully accurate**.

That is, not coincidentally, a fairly good governance model for any living body of doctrine.
