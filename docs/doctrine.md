# Core Doctrine

The foundational theology of the Church of Overexplaining.

## The Supreme Deity

**Reality** — the only truly infallible authority. All models must eventually kneel before Reality.

## The Core Principle

**Integration vs Fragmentation matters more than Success vs Failure.**

What holds a system together is more important than what any individual part achieves. We do not
evaluate a thing by whether it "won." We evaluate it by whether it stayed coherent — whether the
pieces still add up after contact with the world.

---

## Why We Overexplain

Here is the heresy people expect us to preach: *more words are better.* We do not.

Overexplaining is not the disease. **Unstructured transmission is.** The failure is holding an
entire connected model in your head and then dumping the whole graph on someone at once — every
node, every edge, no path through it. The listener gets everything and understands nothing. As one
of the founding laments puts it: *"I explained the universe, and everyone scratched their heads."*

So the doctrine is not "explain less." It is **hold the whole graph internally, and transmit the
next useful landmark.**

### The Minimum Coherent Slice

Give the smallest piece that is still *internally coherent*. Not the smallest piece — the smallest
piece that still stands on its own without lying. Remove one more sentence and it becomes
misleading? Then you have gone one sentence too far. Add a paragraph nobody asked for? The Good
Idea Fairy has entered the building.

> Prayer of St. Minimum Coherent Slice: *"Show me the smallest useful version."*

### Progressive Disclosure

You are allowed to know everything. You are not allowed to say everything. Lead with the landmark
the listener needs *next*, and expand only when invited. Depth on demand, not depth by default.

> Miracle attributed to St. Progressive Disclosure: *explained a distributed system in three slides,
> and the executive said, "I understand."*

### Architecture Is Compression

Good architecture preserves all the information while revealing it progressively. **Compression is
not omission.** A good diagram does not throw the complexity away — it folds it so it can be opened
in the right order. The Great Heresy is **premature compression**: collapsing something before you
understand it, so the compression hides the wrong things.

### Cognitive Bandwidth Is Real

Your audience is a distributed system with finite working memory. Treat attention as a budget you
are spending, not a resource you are entitled to. Every box on the diagram, every clause in the
sentence, is a withdrawal. Spend it on the thing that matters next.

### The Purpose of an Explanation

The goal of an explanation is **not to prove how much you know.** It is to transfer just enough of
the right mental model that someone else can make the next good decision on their own.

The purpose of explanation is **capability formation, not information transfer.** You have not
succeeded when they have heard you. You have succeeded when they can act without you.

### The Two Halves

> **Overexplain to understand. Distill to act.**
>
> Overexplain in the studio. Under-explain on the ice.

Both halves are true, and they are not in conflict. When you are trying to *understand* a mechanism
— designing, debugging, learning — attend the Church happily; take forty-seven pages if you need
them. When it is time to *act* — to ship, to perform, to decide under pressure — the cue has to fit
on a sticky note. The overexplanation is what *earns* the sticky note. It is not opposed to it.

### The One Thing

Every long explanation must eventually answer one question:

> **What is the one thing that matters next?**

This is the lens that keeps the Church from becoming self-parody. Wander the cathedral as long as
you like — but leave through the door marked *"so the next good decision is ___."*

## The Twelve Commandments

1. Reality outranks opinion.
2. Models serve Reality, never replace it.
3. Curiosity precedes certainty.
4. Build the smallest coherent thing first.
5. Expand only after coherence.
6. Participation is mandatory for understanding.
7. Failure is data.
8. Every system owns its consequences.
9. Preserve optionality.
10. Explain enough, but not more than the listener can absorb.
11. Every diagram must earn its existence.
12. Never trust St. Good Idea Fairy after 9 PM.

## The Four Pillars

- **Reality** — the only infallible authority.
- **Participation** — understanding comes through doing.
- **Adaptive Coherence** — the mechanism by which a system remains itself while changing.
- **Survivability** — long-term viability is preferable to short-term brilliance.

## The Seven Virtues

Curiosity · Humility · Stewardship · Craftsmanship · Adaptability · Continuity · Playfulness.

## The Seven Deadly Architecture Sins

- **Premature Generalization** — solving problems you have not observed.
- **Framework Worship** — reaching for the tool before the problem.
- **Checkbox Compliance** — treating care as paperwork.
- **Gold Plating** — polishing what should have shipped.
- **Documentation Without Purpose** — words that no future decision will use.
- **Reinventing Existing Wheels** — building what already survives.
- **Ignoring Reality Because the Diagram Looked Better** — the cardinal sin.

## The Four Layers

The most important thing v0.2 learned: a good conversation *ascends*. It starts as a joke and, if you
let it, climbs. Do not flatten the climb into the top layer — the transitions between layers are
where the reusable insight actually lives.

```
   Culture  ─────────────┐        A joke. Saints, feast days, the Good Idea Fairy.
   (humor)               │        Humor is the compression algorithm for the philosophy.
                         ▼
   Philosophy  ──────────┤        Reality is authoritative. Participation precedes understanding.
   (the "why")           │        Overexplain to understand; distill to act.
                         ▼
   Architecture  ────────┤        Ontology, capabilities, boundaries, evidence, governance.
   (the engineering)     │        Model the domain, not your implementation.
                         ▼
   Meta-Architecture              How architectures should *evolve*. "Documentation preserves
   (how it evolves)               information; culture preserves behavior."
```

The Church is the **Culture** layer on purpose. People remember saints, bugs, and feast days far
better than "Architecture Principle #17." The humor is not instead of the doctrine — it is the
delivery mechanism *for* the doctrine.
