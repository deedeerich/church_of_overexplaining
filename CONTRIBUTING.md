# How to Submit New Doctrine

The Church of Overexplaining is a living canon. It grows through practice, observation, and Reality proving us wrong.

## The Canonization Process

A principle becomes doctrine when:

1. **It appears independently in three separate conversations**
   - Observed in practice, not invented in a meeting

2. **It survives contact with Reality**
   - You've tested it, it works

3. **It remains useful six months later**
   - Not a one-off insight

4. **Everyone laughs because it's painfully accurate**
   - Truth first, humor second

## Types of Doctrine We Accept

- New Saints (canonized principles)
- New Heresies (doctrinal rejections with reasoning)
- New Proverbs (pithy truths)
- New Holidays (sacred observances)
- Amendments to Existing Doctrine (when Reality corrects us)

## The Process

1. **Open an Issue** with your proposal, including evidence
2. **The Council Discusses** — we'll ask questions
3. **Submit a PR** with the new doctrine
4. **Merge and Canonize** — added to the Book of Revisions

## Guidelines

✅ Submit things that are funny because true  
✅ Include evidence from real experience  
✅ Challenge existing doctrine (with evidence)  
❌ Submit theoretical ideas without testing  
❌ Include private or proprietary information
